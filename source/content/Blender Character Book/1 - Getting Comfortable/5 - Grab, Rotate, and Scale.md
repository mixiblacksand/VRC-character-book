The three basic transformations are Grab, Rotate, and Scale. Open a new scene by going to File > New > General.
## Grab
### Video: Grab

![](https://youtu.be/gq5xV3aNa1o)

---

```
Grab : G
Lock Axis : X, Y, or Z
Lock Plane : Shift-X, Shift-Y, or Shift-Z
Interactive Lock : Hold MMB while transforming
Numerical Entry : Type a value and press Enter
Reset Location : Alt-G
```
	Click on the cube and press `G`. Now as you move your mouse, the cube will stick to it, sliding along the plane of the camera. Click `LMB` to accept its new position, or `RMB` to cancel the operation.

What if you want to move it only upwards along the Z axis? To do this, press `G`, then `Z`. A blue line will appear, indicating the axis lock. Likewise you can press `X` or `Y` to lock to those axes. 

To lock to a plane instead of a single axis, press `Shift-X`, `Shift-Y`, or `Shift-Z`. Those keys will lock the movement to the plane of the other two axes.

You can interactively lock the axis by holding `MMB`, hovering over the desired axis, and letting go of `MMB`.

If you want to move the cube a precise distance, you can type in a number and press Enter. For instance, selecting the cube and typing `G`, `X`, `2.5`, `Enter` will move the cube along the X axis 2.5 meters. You don't need to input this into a text box anywhere - just press the keys over the 3D viewport and it will work. 

To return the cube to its default location at the center of the world, press `Alt-G`.

Select the cube and press `R`.  It will rotate around the axis of the screen. Just like with Grab, you can select a specific axis to rotate around with the keyboard or `MMB`.

To rotate the cube freely, press `R` twice.

To reorient the cube to its default state, press `Alt-R`.

Select the cube again and press `S`. The cube scales uniformly from its center point. Select an axis, and it will only scale along that axis.

To clear the scaling, press `Alt-S`.
