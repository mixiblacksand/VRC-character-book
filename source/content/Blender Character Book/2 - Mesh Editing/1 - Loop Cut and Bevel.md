## Loop Cut
```
Loop Cut : Ctrl-R
```
In Edit Mode, hover over a edge of your cube and press Ctrl-R.
![[Pasted image 20260331151943.png]]
This starts the loop cut tool. You can scroll the mouse wheel to increase and decrease the number of cuts. Click `LMB` once to confirm the number of cuts. You can then slide them along the surface and click `LMB` to confirm again, or press `RMB` to cancel sliding and leave them centered along the surface. 

The cut happens perpendicular to the edge your mouse is over. The cut will continue along loops of faces as best it can, but certain geometry can stop it.

## Bevel
```
Bevel : Ctrl-B
```
You can round out or chamfer edges with the Bevel tool. Select some edges and press `Ctrl-B`, then drag the mouse.
![[Pasted image 20260331153730.png]]
Again you can use the mouse wheel to adjust the number of cuts. The [[4 - Working with Objects#The Redo Menu|Redo Menu]] for Bevel contains some options worth playing with, including custom profiles.
