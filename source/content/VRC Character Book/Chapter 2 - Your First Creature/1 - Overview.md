In this chapter we're going to go on a crash course from blank Blender file to working VRC avatar. That sounds like a lot, but we're going to aim for a minimal viable product here - something VRC accepts and we can run around in, but not necessarily something with all the features or that's even _pretty._

My experience learning 3D was that every stage of the process informs previous stages. My modeling improved by understanding UV mapping. My retopo improved by understanding rigging and skinning. My texturing improved by seeing the result in a game engine. Et cetera. 

When we're done, you should have context for what the avatar creation process is like. I think that will boost your understanding much more than studying the details of any one process too soon.
